Introduction

Large language models (LLMs) are a type of artificial intelligence (AI) technology. They are used in various applications such as natural language processing, machine translation, and more.[1]

History

The development of large language models has evolved significantly over the years. Early models were based on simpler statistical methods.[2]

Applications

LLMs are widely used in applications like text generation, linguistic research, and automated customer service.[3]

Technical Details

These models are typically based on neural network architectures that enable them to capture the complexities of human language.[4]

Challenges

Despite their capabilities, LLMs face challenges such as bias, high computational costs, and data privacy concerns.[5]

Future Directions

Research continues to improve LLMs, focusing on reducing biases, enhancing efficiency, and expanding their usability.[6]
